Etymology & Concept

  • The name “イーライ (Iirai)” stretches “Eli” into a delayed echo, like a voice arriving late from a metal tunnel.
  • Nicknamed 怪人ハサミ (Kaijin Hasami, “Scissor Kaijin”), he is treated like a harmless urban legend inside Eli’s projects: a bass‑voiced trickster who cuts out awkward silences and bad takes instead of anything violent.

Visual Design

  • Messy orange hair, freckled face, big green eyes, and a slightly sleep‑deprived grin that makes him look like someone who has been up for three nights working on a single track.
  • Steampunk goggles, oversized scissors, gear motifs on his pants, tool belts and straps, and a patched shirt and vest—somewhere between mechanic, tailor, and ritual assistant.

Personality & Trivia

  • Talks fast, rambles about strange ideas, and bursts into glitched laughter, which contrasts with his deep, bone‑shaking singing voice.
  • Treats bad tuning and strange EQ as “timeline slips,” insisting that the project only needs one tiny cut to sound right again.
  • Rumours say his giant scissors can snip sounds and moments out of the air and paste them back as jump‑cuts in conversations or déjà‑vu loops in songs.

Voicebank Characterisation

  • Japanese CVVC voicebank focused on a low, warm male register with a slightly rough texture and eerie atmosphere, designed to sit between “human” and “glitchy ritual” in mixes.
  • Works especially well in dark folk, industrial, and experimental tracks that leave room for bass resonance and long notes.
